title: "Locating the gamma-ray emission region in the brightest Fermi-LAT flat-spectrum radio quasars"
collection: publications
category: manuscripts
permalink: /publication/acharyya_2021_mnras_fsqr
date: 2021-02-04
venue: "Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, 500(4), 5297–5321"
paperurl: "https://doi.org/10.1093/mnras/staa3483"
excerpt: |
  We conducted a detailed study of the gamma-ray emission from some of the brightest flat-spectrum radio quasars (FSRQs) detected by the Fermi Large Area Telescope over its first 8 years. Our goal was to understand where in the jet the gamma-ray emission originates. By analyzing the most intense flares from each quasar, we observed rapid changes in brightness over short time scales (less than an hour) for some sources. This suggests that the gamma-ray emission comes from very compact regions in the jet, possibly within the area around the black hole’s accretion disk, known as the broad-line region (BLR).
  
  Additionally, we found that the flare spectra showed signs of a "cut-off" in the energy of the gamma rays in most of the flares we studied, which supports the idea that these emissions could come from the BLR. We also looked into how long it takes for particles to cool in these regions and found evidence that the emissions might come from both the BLR and a surrounding region called the molecular torus (MT). However, our simulations showed that some of the sources had gamma-ray emissions that couldn't be explained by the BLR, pointing to the fact that the gamma rays might come from multiple compact regions throughout the jet. Our findings suggest that the brightest FSRQs have gamma-ray emissions originating from various regions, both in the BLR and the MT, within their jets.

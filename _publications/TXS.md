excerpt: |
  In September 2017, a high-energy neutrino event detected by the IceCube Neutrino Observatory (IceCube-170922A) was associated, at the 3σ level, with a gamma-ray flare from the blazar 51 TXS 0506+056. Cosmic rays accelerated in astrophysical sources can escape their jets and interact with background radiation, generating pions that decay into neutrinos and gamma-rays. We report on a search for gamma-ray emission from such cosmogenic cascades using Fermi-LAT and VERITAS data. Our analysis compares the observed spectra with the predictions for cosmic-ray induced cascades in intergalactic space, providing constraints on the proton luminosity and the escape mechanisms in this source.

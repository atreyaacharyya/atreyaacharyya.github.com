date: 2017-09-01
venue: "The Astrophysical Journal, 851(2), 120"

excerpt: |
  In September 2017, a high-energy neutrino event detected by the IceCube Neutrino Observatory (IceCube-170922A) was associated, at the 3σ level, with a gamma-ray flare from the blazar 51 TXS 0506+056. Cosmic rays accelerated in astrophysical sources can interact with background radiation fields, producing pions that generate neutrinos and inverse Compton scattering leading to secondary gamma-ray emission detectable by telescopes. 
  
  We present a new search for cosmogenic cascade emission from TXS 0506+056, using data from the Fermi-Large Area Telescope and VERITAS. The gamma-ray spectrum and neutrino observations are compared to predictions of cosmic-ray induced cascades in intergalactic space. The observed spectrum is modeled as a combination of the primary and cascade spectra. A Monte Carlo simulation with a likelihood analysis is applied to determine the best-fit parameters of the proton emission spectrum.
  
  The results show consistency with a proton injection spectral index of αp ≃ 2.0 and a cutoff energy of Ep,max ≃ 1.3 × 10¹⁶ eV. We constrain the isotropic proton escape luminosity to 1×10⁴⁴ erg s⁻¹ ≲ Lp,esc ≲ 3×10⁴⁵ erg s⁻¹ at the 90% confidence level.

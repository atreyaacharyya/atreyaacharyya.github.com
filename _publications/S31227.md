title: "Detection of Very High Energy Gamma-Ray Emission from Blazar S3 1227+25"
collection: publications
category: manuscripts
permalink: /publication/acharyya_2015_s3_1227
date: 2015-05-16
venue: "The Astrophysical Journal, 815(2), 118"
paperurl: "https://doi.org/10.1088/0004-637X/815/2/118"
excerpt: |
  We report the detection of very high energy gamma-ray emission from the blazar S3 1227+25 (VER J1230+253) with the VERITAS telescope array. The detection was triggered by a hard-spectrum GeV flare observed by the Fermi-Large Area Telescope on 2015 May 15. A combined 5-hour VERITAS exposure on May 16 and 18 resulted in a 13σ detection with a differential photon spectral index of Γ = 3.8 ± 0.4 and a flux level of 9% of the Crab Nebula above 120 GeV.
  
  This flare triggered additional multiwavelength observations with Swift, optical photometry, polarimetry, and radio measurements. A temporal analysis revealed evidence of a shortest variability timescale of τobs = 6.2 ± 0.9 hr, suggesting emission from compact regions within the jet. The combined gamma-ray spectrum showed no significant spectral cutoff.
  
  We found evidence of optical and gamma-ray correlations, implying a single-zone emission model. The multiwavelength spectral energy distribution is well described by a one-zone leptonic synchrotron self-Compton model, providing further insights into the emission mechanism.

title: "Investigating Internal Absorption Features in Gamma-Ray Emission from High Redshift Flat Spectrum Radio Quasars"
collection: publications

date: 2021-01-01
venue: "The Astrophysical Journal, 910(1), 105"
paperurl: "https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/abe99e"
excerpt: |
  The gamma-ray emission from Flat Spectrum Radio Quasars (FSRQs), a subclass of blazars, is believed to result from interactions between high-energy leptons and/or hadrons in the jet with ambient photon fields, such as those from the accretion disk, the broad-line region (BLR), and the dusty torus. However, these photon fields can also attenuate gamma-rays via internal photon-photon absorption, imprinting characteristic features in the spectrum.
  
  This study investigates internal absorption in FSRQs, focusing on a sample of high-redshift (z >= 3) gamma-ray detected sources. We model the gamma-ray spectra from Fermi-LAT observations using a detailed gamma-gamma opacity model, assuming that the BLR photon field dominates the absorption. We specifically focus on the energy range ~25 GeV/(1+z), where absorption features due to Lyα photons are expected. Our analysis identifies a potential internal absorption feature in the lowest redshift object in our sample (z ~ 3) and provides constraints on the location of its gamma-ray emission region along the jet.
  
  For the remaining higher-redshift sources, photon statistics are insufficient to detect absorption features, highlighting the need for more sensitive observations to fully understand the internal structure and emission mechanisms in FSRQs.

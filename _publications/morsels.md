excerpt: |
  We investigate the possibility of probing the quantum nature of black holes through gamma-ray observations, with the aim of identifying signatures consistent with Hawking radiation. By combining theoretical modeling with high-energy astrophysical data, we explore whether gamma-ray emission from compact objects can provide indirect evidence for quantum gravitational effects.

  Our study examines the spectral and temporal characteristics expected from Hawking radiation in astrophysical environments and compares them with observational constraints. We discuss the implications for current and future gamma-ray observatories and highlight potential strategies to distinguish such signals from conventional astrophysical processes. These results open a pathway toward testing fundamental predictions of quantum gravity using high-energy observations.

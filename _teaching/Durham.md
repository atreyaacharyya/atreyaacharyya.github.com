I served as a Physics Tutor and Teaching Assistant at Durham University, delivering small-group tutorials to first-year undergraduate students in the Department of Physics (2018–2021).

##  Responsibilities
Planning and delivering conceptual tutorials for groups of approximately six students
Guiding students through problem sets and developing their physical intuition
Supporting students in understanding core undergraduate topics in physics
Marking weekly homework assignments and progress tests

##  Additional Teaching Contributions
Delivered one-to-one and drop-in tutoring sessions in calculus, linear algebra, and complex numbers
Assisted students in applying mathematical methods to physics problems
Supported programming-related problem solving in Python and related tools

##  Mentoring
Provided pastoral and academic support to undergraduate physics students
Helped students manage workload and adapt to university-level study

These roles strengthened my experience in small-group teaching, assessment, and student mentoring in a research-led academic environment.

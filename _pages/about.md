I am an astrophysicist specializing in high-energy and multi-messenger studies of the universe, with a particular focus on gamma-ray astronomy. I obtained my PhD from Durham University, where my research focused on understanding the physics of extreme astrophysical environments through very high energy gamma-ray observations.

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at University of Southern Denmark, working at CP3-Origins on an ERC-funded project. My research focuses on probing the origin of gamma-ray emission in Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) using data from the Fermi Large Area Telescope and ground-based observatories including VERITAS Observatory and H.E.S.S. Collaboration.

My work combines observational data analysis, theoretical modeling, and multi-wavelength approaches to understand particle acceleration and emission processes in some of the most energetic systems in the universe. I am particularly interested in multi-messenger connections between gamma rays, neutrinos, and cosmic rays.

I play an active role in large international collaborations, including serving as co-chair of the CTAO AGN Population Task Force and previously co-chairing the VERITAS Blazar Working Group. My research also includes population studies of extragalactic gamma-ray sources, developing statistical frameworks to understand their demographics and evolution at very high energies.

I am involved in the development and scientific preparation of the Cherenkov Telescope Array Observatory, contributing to both analysis methodologies and software tools for the next generation of gamma-ray observations. I have also contributed as a Co-Principal Investigator on multi-messenger projects funded by NASA and NSF.

In addition to research, I am engaged in teaching and mentoring, delivering lectures in astroparticle physics and supervising students at undergraduate and postgraduate levels.

Overall, my work aims to use extreme astrophysical environments as laboratories to probe fundamental physics and better understand the high-energy universe.
